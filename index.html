The South Carolina AI Observatory (SCAIO) is an independent, non-partisan hub established to aggregate disparate resources and provide rigorous technical oversight.

Our mission is to monitor the evolution of AI within South Carolina, providing data-driven clarity to navigate the complexities of ethics, security, and economic impact.

Founding Authority

Jimmy Ardis

Executive Director

Jimmy Ardis is a veteran policy analyst and community development expert who specializes in assessing the impact of emerging trends on vulnerable populations and municipal infrastructure.

Beginning his career managing emergency response and crash-recovery equipment in the United States Air Force [cite: 562, 563], Ardis later served as a Policy Analyst for the Joseph P. Riley Jr. Center for Livable Communities[cite: 549, 550]. There, he developed resiliency frameworks and utilized economic, demographic, and GIS analysis to craft public policy directly adopted into the Charleston City Comprehensive Plan[cite: 553, 554, 596].

As a Staff Writer for Ballotpedia, he published over 300 data-driven articles on local government and public policy[cite: 555, 559]. Ardis built and scaled the consultancy Civitas, directing community development and HUD compliance across nine states[cite: 529, 532, 546]. He is the founder of the AI automation agency Carolina Redesign and continues to advise state and local governments through The Commons Group[cite: 522, 526, 528].

Noah Schiffman

Chief Technical Advisor

Noah Schiffman is a distinguished authority in IT security with over two decades of experience protecting corporate, defense, and intelligence assets from cyber-attacks[cite: 388, 390, 391].

Holding a DoD Top Secret clearance, Schiffman recently served as the Chief Technology Advisor for KBR, a Fortune 500 global provider of science and engineering solutions[cite: 394, 395, 417]. Throughout his career, he has operated as a "white-hat" hacker and Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) for organizations like Wave Sciences and Orbis, Inc., managing advanced penetration testing, biometric security integration, and vulnerability analysis[cite: 396, 400, 429, 430].

A true polymath, Schiffman holds an M.D. from the Medical University of South Carolina, degrees in Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering and Cognitive Psychology from Rutgers University, and multiple patents in acoustic and medical device technologies[cite: 392, 409, 413, 415, 492]. He is a regular speaker at national hacking and security conferences, including Defcon and BSides[cite: 471, 472].
